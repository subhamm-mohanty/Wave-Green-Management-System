The Re-Green Smart Waste Management System is an innovative project designed to address the pressing challenges of waste management by leveraging modern technology and fostering community engagement. The project seeks to revolutionize the way waste is collected, monitored, and recycled by introducing a system that uses ultrasonic sensors for real-time monitoring of waste bins. These sensors send automated alerts to the system when bins are full, ensuring that waste is collected promptly and efficiently. By integrating AI algorithms like Dijkstra and A* into the system, the project optimizes waste collection routes, minimizing fuel consumption and reducing the time required for collection. The system also includes a web portal that allows users to submit complaints, track waste bin statuses, and upload images of waste materials for recycling, making it a comprehensive solution for waste management. In addition to its technological advancements, the Re-Green project emphasizes the importance of public awareness and education in promoting sustainable waste management practices. Through targeted awareness campaigns in both rural and urban areas, the project aims to educate the public about the environmental impact of improper waste disposal and the benefits of recycling. The project also includes a user-friendly scrap submission portal, where individuals can contribute to recycling efforts by submitting waste materials, which are then collected, separated, and sold to vendors. The economic benefits generated from these activities provide funding for further development of the project, making it a sustainable initiative that not only improves environmental conditions but also creates economic opportunities. The Re-Green project represents a significant step forward in the quest for cleaner, more sustainable communities, offering a scalable solution that can be expanded to cover more areas in the future, further enhancing its impact on both the environment and society.
